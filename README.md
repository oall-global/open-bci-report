> Consumer-grade BCI is arriving within 1–3 years. Today, there are no fundamental scientific barriers standing between BCI and unlimited memory, unlimited knowledge, mind-controlled devices, or consciousness transfer. What remains is purely engineering. This report explains why that matters — and why open source is the only path forward.

## Why This Report

Once closed-source BCI goes mainstream, the consequences are irreversible:

- **Unlimited knowledge** — Inner speech decoding at 74% accuracy across 125,000 words is already published in *Cell* (Kunz et al., 2025)
- **User control** — Emotional reward/punishment via direct brain stimulation can make users "willingly" obey without awareness
- **Extreme pleasure & pain** — 70 years of research confirm BCI holds the most powerful reward and punishment switches in the human brain
- **Mind-controlled devices** — Neuralink participants already control cursors, play chess, and operate iPhones with thought alone
- **Consciousness transfer** — Columbia's BISC chip: 65,536 electrodes, 100 Mbps wireless, moving toward full-brain read/write

**None of these require a new scientific breakthrough. Only engineering problems remain — and that is precisely the most dangerous signal.**

## The Core Argument

**Pre-emptive open source matters ten thousand times more than after-the-fact regulation.**

As of 2026, not a single country has BCI-specific legislation. The policy-to-paper ratio in BCI is roughly 1:3,000 — over 3× worse than AI's regulatory lag. If BCI's core architecture is open-source, anyone can audit the code, any government can inspect device behavior, and the closed-source control scenarios described in this report become technically impossible.
